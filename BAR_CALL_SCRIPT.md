# Bar Call Script — Golden Hour

Use this when calling State College bars to collect happy hour info. Keep it casual and short — you're not pitching anything, just asking for public information.

---

## Opening

> "Hey, is this [Bar Name]? Great. I have a quick question — do you guys do happy hour?"

If yes:
> "Awesome. I'm putting together a local happy hour guide for State College and wanted to make sure I had your info right. It'll only take a minute."

If no:
> "Got it, thanks for letting me know!" — hang up, mark as no happy hour.

---

## Core Questions

Ask these in order. Fill in the form below while they talk.

1. **What days do you have happy hour?**
   > "What days do you run it?"

2. **What are the hours?**
   > "And what time does it run — like 4 to 7, something like that?"

3. **What are the deals?**
   > "What kind of deals do you have during happy hour? Like specific prices or items?"
   
   Follow-up if vague:
   > "Is it like drink specials, food specials, or both?"

4. **Any restrictions?**
   > "Is it dine-in only, or does it apply at the bar too?"

5. **Anything change on weekends?**
   > "Does the happy hour change at all on weekends, or is it the same all week?"

---

## Closing

> "Perfect, that's all I needed. Thanks so much — I'll make sure your info is accurate."

If they seem curious:
> "It's a local app that helps people find happy hour deals around State College. We're just trying to make sure everything is correct before we publish it."

## Tips

- **Best time to call**: 2–4pm weekdays. Before the rush, after lunch service.
- **If they can't talk**: "No problem, I can call back — what's a better time?"
- **If they don't know**: Ask for a manager or bartender. The bartender usually knows every deal by heart.
- **If the deal is complicated**: Ask them to slow down and repeat it. Don't guess.
- **If they have a website**: Note it — verify later that the site matches what they told you.
- **If they seem skeptical**: You're not selling anything. Just say you're building a free local resource.

## If They Have No Happy Hour

Options they might use instead:
- "Specials" (same thing, different name)
- "Daily deals" or "daily features"
- Industry night (usually Sunday or Monday — ask if applicable)
- Late-night specials (11pm+)

Always ask: *"Do you have any drink or food specials at any point during the week?"*
